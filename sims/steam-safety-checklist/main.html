Check boiler water level
Verify water level is visible in the sight glass at normal operating level. If level is low, check feedwater system. If level is not visible, do NOT start the boiler.
Test low water cutoff (float type)
Slowly lower the water level by opening the blowdown valve until the LWCO trips and shuts down the burner. Restore water level immediately. Document the trip level.
Check steam pressure
Compare operating pressure gauge reading to set point. Verify pressure is stable and within normal range. Investigate any unusual pressure fluctuations.
Observe flame through sight glass
Flame should be stable, centered in the firebox, and have a consistent shape. Yellow tips indicate insufficient air. Lifting or pulsating flame indicates combustion problems.
Check feedwater pump operation
Verify pump is running smoothly, no unusual noise or vibration. Check suction and discharge pressures. Verify feedwater temperature at deaerator outlet.
Log boiler room temperatures and pressures
Record steam pressure, feedwater temperature, stack temperature, boiler room ambient temperature, and makeup water meter reading in the daily log.
Bottom blowdown
Open the bottom blowdown valve for 5-10 seconds to remove sludge and sediment from the mud drum. Perform at low-fire or when load permits. Do NOT leave unattended.
Test low water cutoff (probe type)
Simulate a low water condition by interrupting the probe signal. Verify the burner shuts down within the required time. Reset and verify normal operation.
Check chemical feed pumps and levels
Verify chemical feed pumps are operating and calibrated. Check chemical tank levels. Reorder chemicals if below 25% capacity.
Inspect condensate return tank
Check condensate receiver tank level and temperature. Investigate any unusual color, odor, or oil contamination in the condensate.
Analyze boiler water chemistry
Test boiler water for TDS, pH, sulfite residual, phosphate, alkalinity, and silica. Compare to treatment program limits. Adjust chemical feed rates as needed.
Inspect and clean flame scanner
Remove and clean the flame scanner lens. Check alignment. Verify flame signal strength is within manufacturer specifications.
Check burner condition
Inspect burner tip for soot buildup or damage. Check diffuser and air register for proper positioning. Clean as needed.
Inspect deaerator operation
Verify vent plume is visible (indicates proper deaeration). Check spray valves or tray condition. Verify operating temperature matches pressure.
Internal inspection
Coordinate with insurance inspector. Lock out/tag out and cool down boiler. Open manways. Inspect waterside for scale, corrosion, and pitting. Inspect fireside for soot, refractory damage.
Safety valve certification
Have safety relief valves tested and certified by an authorized valve repair facility. Replace gaskets and reseat as needed. Verify set pressure matches nameplate.
Calibrate all safety controls
Calibrate low water cutoff, high pressure limit, and all operating controls. Document calibration values. Replace any devices that fail calibration.
