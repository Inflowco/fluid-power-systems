Float & Thermostatic (F&T) Trap

Operating Principle: Density (mechanical)

A ball float rises as condensate fills the trap body, opening a valve to discharge condensate continuously. A separate thermostatic air vent at the top releases air and non-condensable gases. The most versatile trap type for process applications.

Best for: Process equipment, variable loads
Air handling: Excellent (thermostatic vent)
Discharge: Continuous
Durability: Good — float can be damaged by water hammer

Inverted Bucket Trap

Operating Principle: Density (mechanical)

An inverted bucket inside the trap body floats when steam enters (closing the valve) and sinks when condensate fills it (opening the valve). Requires a water seal to operate. Extremely durable and resistant to water hammer.

Best for: Main drip legs, steady loads
Air handling: Fair — limited by small vent hole
Discharge: Intermittent (blast discharge)
Durability: Excellent — simple, rugged design

Thermodynamic (Disc) Trap

Operating Principle: Velocity and pressure

A flat disc sits on a seat. When condensate enters, it lifts the disc and flows through. Flash steam from hot condensate creates a low-pressure zone above the disc, pulling it down to close. When flash steam condenses, the disc lifts again.

Best for: Drip legs, steam tracing
Air handling: Fair
Discharge: Intermittent (cyclic click)
Durability: Good — only one moving part

Balanced Pressure (Thermostatic) Trap

Operating Principle: Temperature

A fluid-filled capsule expands when heated by steam (closing the valve) and contracts as condensate cools below steam temperature (opening the valve). Provides excellent air venting and is self-adjusting to different pressures.

Best for: Light loads, air venting, tracing
Air handling: Excellent — opens wide on startup
Discharge: Modulating (opens as condensate cools)
Durability: Moderate — capsule sensitive to water hammer

Diagnostic Challenge

Evaluate each trap's readings and diagnose its condition.

Trap #1 (Inverted bucket on 125 psig header drip)
Upstream temp: 353°F | Downstream temp: 348°F | Ultrasonic: Continuous loud rushing
Continuous loud rushing sound with near-equal temperatures upstream and downstream indicates live steam passing through — failed open.
Trap #2 (F&T on heat exchanger at 50 psig)
Upstream temp: 298°F | Downstream temp: 220°F | Ultrasonic: Intermittent gurgling
Temperature drop downstream and intermittent gurgling are normal for a working F&T trap discharging condensate — working correctly.
Trap #3 (Disc trap on steam tracing at 100 psig)
Upstream temp: 190°F | Downstream temp: 140°F | Ultrasonic: Silent
Upstream temperature far below saturation (338°F at 100 psig) with silence indicates condensate is backing up — failed closed.
