    Liquid Ring Vacuum Pump System — Project Timeline

    Project Duration: February 24, 2025 – March 17, 2026 (386 days) Scope: 8 liquid ring vacuum pumps, 4 rotating pressure taps, leak detection, system layout, thermotemp probes

    Project Summary

    Parameter Value
    Vacuum Pumps 8
    Pressure Tap Sets 4 (portable, rotating)
    Tap Rotation Interval Every 5 days
    Total Rotation Cycles 77
    Total Tap-Location Data Points 308
    Thermotemp Probes Continuous on all 8 pumps

    Phase 1 — Mobilization & System Layout (Feb 24 – Mar 14, 2025)

    Duration: 3 weeks

    Week Dates Activity
    1 Feb 24 – Feb 28 Site mobilization, safety orientation, equipment staging
    1 Feb 24 – Feb 28 Walk-down all 8 pumps — document nameplate data, piping, and controls
    2 Mar 3 – Mar 7 System layout — create P&ID / isometric drawings of vacuum header and branch lines
    2 Mar 3 – Mar 7 Identify and tag all pressure tap locations (suction, discharge, seal water, inter-stage)
    3 Mar 10 – Mar 14 Install thermotemp probes on all 8 pumps (seal water in/out, discharge gas temp)
    3 Mar 10 – Mar 14 Commission data loggers, verify communication, set sample rates

    Deliverables:

    • [ ] System layout drawings (P&ID, isometrics)
    • [ ] Pump nameplate and baseline condition log
    • [ ] Tap location map with IDs
    • [ ] Data logger commissioning report

    Phase 2 — Data Logging & Pressure Tap Rotation (Mar 17, 2025 – Jan 30, 2026)

    Duration: ~46 weeks (320 days) — 64 rotation cycles

    Pressure Tap Rotation Schedule

    Each pump has 4 tap locations (suction, discharge, seal water supply, seal water return). With 4 portable pressure tap sets rotating every 5 days across 8 pumps:

    • Taps per rotation: 4 simultaneous locations
    • Rotations to cover all 8 pumps × 4 locations = 32 points: 8 rotations (40 days)
    • Complete survey cycles over 320 days: ~8 full survey passes

    Survey Cycle Pattern (40 days per complete pass)

    Rotation Days Tap Set A Tap Set B Tap Set C Tap Set D
    1 1–5 Pump 1 Suction Pump 1 Discharge Pump 1 Seal In Pump 1 Seal Out
    2 6–10 Pump 2 Suction Pump 2 Discharge Pump 2 Seal In Pump 2 Seal Out
    3 11–15 Pump 3 Suction Pump 3 Discharge Pump 3 Seal In Pump 3 Seal Out
    4 16–20 Pump 4 Suction Pump 4 Discharge Pump 4 Seal In Pump 4 Seal Out
    5 21–25 Pump 5 Suction Pump 5 Discharge Pump 5 Seal In Pump 5 Seal Out
    6 26–30 Pump 6 Suction Pump 6 Discharge Pump 6 Seal In Pump 6 Seal Out
    7 31–35 Pump 7 Suction Pump 7 Discharge Pump 7 Seal In Pump 7 Seal Out
    8 36–40 Pump 8 Suction Pump 8 Discharge Pump 8 Seal In Pump 8 Seal Out

    Full Survey Pass Schedule

    Pass Start Date End Date Notes
    Pass 1 Mar 17, 2025 Apr 25, 2025 Baseline survey — establish reference data
    Pass 2 Apr 28, 2025 Jun 6, 2025 Spring operating conditions
    Pass 3 Jun 9, 2025 Jul 18, 2025 Summer load — peak cooling water temps
    Pass 4 Jul 21, 2025 Aug 29, 2025 Summer peak — highest seal water temps
    Pass 5 Sep 1, 2025 Oct 10, 2025 Fall transition
    Pass 6 Oct 13, 2025 Nov 21, 2025 Fall/winter operating conditions
    Pass 7 Nov 24, 2025 Jan 2, 2026 Winter baseline — coldest seal water temps
    Pass 8 Jan 5, 2026 Jan 30, 2026 Final confirmation survey

    Thermotemp Probe — Continuous Monitoring

    Running on all 8 pumps for the entire data logging phase:

    Measurement Location Purpose
    Seal water inlet temp Each pump supply line Detect cooling water supply issues
    Seal water outlet temp Each pump return line Calculate heat rejection, detect cavitation risk
    Discharge gas temp Pump discharge Monitor compression efficiency
    Delta-T (calculated) Inlet vs. outlet Trending pump degradation

    Phase 3 — Leak Detection Surveys (Quarterly)

    4 comprehensive surveys over the project duration

    Survey Dates Scope
    Survey 1 Apr 14 – Apr 18, 2025 Baseline leak survey — full system
    Survey 2 Jul 14 – Jul 18, 2025 Mid-summer — joints expand at higher temps
    Survey 3 Oct 13 – Oct 17, 2025 Post-summer — check for thermal fatigue leaks
    Survey 4 Jan 19 – Jan 23, 2026 Final survey — verify repairs, new leaks

    Leak Detection Methods

    Method Application
    Ultrasonic detection All vacuum piping joints, flanges, valve packing
    Vacuum decay test Isolatable sections — close valves, monitor pressure rise
    Soap/bubble test Confirmation of suspected leak locations
    Smoke test Large header sections (if system can be isolated)

    Leak Detection Deliverables (per survey)

    • [ ] Leak location map overlaid on system layout
    • [ ] Leak severity classification (critical / moderate / minor)
    • [ ] Estimated air ingress rate (SCFM) per leak
    • [ ] Repair recommendations and priority ranking
    • [ ] Cumulative leak cost estimate (energy impact)

    Phase 4 — Analysis & Reporting (Feb 2 – Mar 17, 2026)

    Duration: 6 weeks

    Week Dates Activity
    1 Feb 2 – Feb 6 Compile and clean all data logging records
    2 Feb 9 – Feb 13 Statistical analysis — pressure profiles, trends, seasonal variation
    3 Feb 16 – Feb 20 Thermotemp analysis — heat rejection, efficiency degradation curves
    4 Feb 23 – Feb 27 Leak detection summary — total air ingress, cost of leaks, repair tracking
    5 Mar 2 – Mar 6 Draft final report — findings, recommendations, ROI for repairs
    6 Mar 9 – Mar 17 Report review, revisions, final delivery

    Final Report Contents

    1. Executive Summary
    2. System Layout Drawings (as-found)
    3. Pump Performance Summary (all 8 pumps, 8 survey passes)
    4. Pressure Profile Analysis by Location
    5. Thermotemp Trending & Efficiency Analysis
    6. Leak Detection Results (4 surveys, cumulative)
    7. Energy Waste Quantification
    8. Recommended Repairs & Capital Improvements
    9. Appendices — Raw Data, Calibration Certificates
